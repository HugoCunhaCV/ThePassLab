What's the difference between confidentiality encryption and a digital signature?
D1 · CRYPTO click to flip
Confidentiality: encrypt with recipient's PUBLIC key (only they can decrypt with private). Digital signature: encrypt hash with sender's PRIVATE key (anyone verifies with public). Direction matters.

05 / honest review

What worked.
What didn't.

Real talk after 8 weeks of trial-and-error. No course is the perfect course. Pick what works.

WHAT WORKED

  • Daily Messer videos in week 1-2, then targeted spot-rewatches
  • LogNPacific daily — random Qs prevented memorization
  • 4-step loop: Read 2x → predict OUT LOUD → paper → rewrite if wrong
  • Per-domain mock scoring drove targeted surgery
  • Weekly cycles: MOCK → 2 days fix → MOCK
  • Building my own cheatsheet (the act of writing locks in memory)
  • Sleep on exam week. Brain consolidates more than cramming
  • Taking ISC2 CC first — gave momentum + a real credential

WHAT DIDN'T

  • Buying multiple courses early — analysis paralysis
  • Trying to memorize specific question banks (Dion mocks rotate)
  • Skipping rest days "just to push through"
  • Studying every domain every day — caused memory-trading
  • Taking mocks without writing wrong answers on paper
  • Trusting one cheatsheet without verifying against Messer
  • Last-minute mock the day before exam — burns confidence
  • Buying TryHackMe paid sub before exam — added zero exam value
